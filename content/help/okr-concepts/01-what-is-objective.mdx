# What is an Objective?

An **Objective** is a clear, inspiring, qualitative statement of what you want to achieve. It answers the question: *"Where do we want to go?"*

<Callout type="tip">
Good Objectives are ambitious but not vague. They should excite the team and create a shared sense of direction.
</Callout>

## Characteristics of a Strong Objective

- **Qualitative** — describes a destination, not a number
- **Ambitious** — stretches the team beyond the comfort zone
- **Time-bound** — scoped to a cycle (quarter, year)
- **Memorable** — short enough to repeat from memory
- **Actionable** — the team can influence the outcome

## Good vs. Weak Examples

| Weak Objective | Strong Objective |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Improve our product" | "Become the most loved project management tool for remote teams" |
| "Grow revenue" | "Establish our presence in the enterprise market" |
| "Fix performance" | "Make our app feel instant for every user" |

## Where Objectives Live in the Hierarchy

In John Doerr's framework (from *Measure What Matters*), Objectives cascade through an organization:

1. **Company** — sets the annual "True North" objectives
2. **Team** — crafts quarterly objectives aligned to company direction
3. **Individual** — defines personal objectives that support team goals

The cascade is **not top-down mandated** — Doerr recommends roughly 50% of objectives bubble up from teams, ensuring ownership and motivation.

<Callout type="warning">
Avoid stacking too many Objectives. 3–5 per cycle is ideal. More than 5 and focus collapses.
</Callout>
