# OKR Cadence

OKRs run on **nested time cycles**. Each level of the organization operates on a cycle length matched to its planning horizon. The cycles interlock so shorter cycles serve the longer ones.

## The Three Cycles

### Annual — Company Objectives

Set at the beginning of each year. These are the "True North" goals that define where the entire organization is going.

- **Who sets them:** Executive team, with broad input
- **How many:** 3–5 company-level Objectives
- **Review cadence:** Mid-year check (not renegotiated, just assessed)
- **Purpose:** Long horizon — market position, product vision, capability building

### Quarterly — Team & Individual OKRs

The primary operational unit. Teams set OKRs that serve the annual company objectives.

- **Who sets them:** Team leads + team members (collaborative)
- **How many:** 2–4 team Objectives, each with 2–4 KRs
- **Review cadence:** Weekly or biweekly check-ins
- **Purpose:** Concrete progress you can measure and act on in 13 weeks

<Callout type="tip">
Start planning next quarter's OKRs 2–3 weeks before the quarter ends. Don't wait until week 1 — you'll lose the first two weeks to setup.
</Callout>

### Weekly — CFR Check-ins

Not a separate OKR cycle, but the operating rhythm that keeps OKRs alive:

- **Monday:** Set weekly priorities tied to OKRs
- **Friday (or async):** Score progress, note blockers, update confidence level
- **1:1s:** CFR conversations tied to current OKR status

## The Planning Sequence

When starting a new cycle:

1. **Company shares** annual OKRs and any updates
2. **Teams draft** quarterly OKRs (50% aligned to company, 50% bottom-up)
3. **Cross-team sync** — resolve dependencies between teams
4. **Lock** — commit to OKRs by end of week 1 of the quarter
5. **Weekly check-ins** begin

<Callout type="warning">
Don't lock OKRs before teams have had a chance to draft bottom-up. Top-down-only OKRs create compliance, not commitment.
</Callout>

## Mid-Cycle Adjustments

OKRs can be modified mid-cycle if circumstances materially change (market shift, product pivot, team change). This is not failure — it's responsible management. Document the reason and reset the baseline.
